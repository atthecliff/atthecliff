We are questioning a lot right now.

Marriage.  
Family.  
Work.  
Relationships.

Much of it comes from exposure—to ideas and lifestyles that didn’t originate here.

What we see and consume carries a certain bias.

And slowly, those ideas begin to feel universal.

In many Western societies, individual independence sits at the center.

From a distance, it can seem cleaner. Even correct.

But context matters.

Every society is shaped by its own history and lived realities.

It’s easy to adopt ideas.

It’s harder to understand what made them work.

If you zoom out, independence does seem natural.

But replacing everything blindly isn’t right either.

The real challenge isn’t choosing between tradition and modernity.

It’s understanding what actually fits your life.
